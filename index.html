Sexual Harassment

This counter demonstrates the constant flow of sexual harassment that women face in America. In the United States of America, a woman is sexually harassed every 0.57 seconds . This happens at work, in universities, on the street, and online. A whopping 82% of U.S. adults over the age of 18 have experienced sexual harassment or assault in their lifetime, and 87% of women did not disclose their experiences to anyone.

The systems in place to help women need drastic improvement, and we can start by raising awareness of these issues. Every number represents a real person with a real experience of harassment. I implore you to consider how these faceless statistics translate into lived experiences of mothers, daughters, and sisters across the country.

Sexual Assault

According to several studies including a meta study evaluating their validity, 1 in 5 women report having been sexually assaulted in their lifetimes. This is a staggering and alarming number of assaults, and studies show that it may not be the whole story. According to two different studies, there could be as few as 15.8%-38% of sexual assaults reported to the police. This means that there could be upwards of 50% of women in the U.S. who experience some form of sexual assault in their lifetime. Sexual assaults are not ‘out of the ordinary’ or ‘exceptional’ cases by any means, despite news outlets only giving attention to “cases involving a celebrity or lurid details…” as told by Rebecca Solnit .

Rape and Abortions

When Roe v. Wade was overturned on June 24, 2022, by the Dobbs v Jackson Women’s Health Organization case, 14 states outright banned abortion for their citizens. In the following 4-18 months that these states held their ban, there were an estimated 64,565 rape-related pregnancies. Staggeringly, only 5 of these states allowed exceptions for rape-related pregnancies, and specific and demanding requirements had to be met.

There is a frightening disparity in the resources provided to victims of sexual assault versus the resources dedicated to stopping women from getting having control over their bodies. The laws surrounding these issues are unjust and must be brought to light to incite change in the government’s policies.

93.5% of Sexual Abusers are Men

Young Men's Education

Women are not exempt from sexual crimes, but they make up a mere fraction of the sexual abuse offenders in the U.S. This disparity highlights a glaring issue in today’s society regarding education: young men are not educated on respectful behaviour. Messaging about rape, assault, and harassment is almost exclusively targeted at the victims (who are primarily women), telling them to carry a whistle, carry pepper spray, take self defense courses.

Any messaging directed towards men regarding sexual assault or the pent-up feelings that may result in violent action would go a long way. A proactive approach to the prevention and education surrounding sexual assault would be extraordinary, starting with the education in schools. In recent years, social media personalities such as Andrew Tate have blown up on the internet, leading young boys and men to misogynistic ideals; teachers are left with precious few resources to combat these ideologies, as curriculums are commonly devoid of these issues.
